# Strategic Site Architecture Summary

## Overall Objective

Create a **primary UX portfolio page** that serves dual roles:

1. **Proof of competence** for UX roles and leadership applications.
2. **Conversion hub** that filters traffic into persona-aligned consulting pages.

These pages must:

- Be easy to skim and navigate
- Immediately establish credibility
- Offer low-friction lead magnets
- Guide each visitor toward the right engagement path based on their needs

## Core Principle: Communicate Leverage in Every Block

Every section must showcase:

### 1. **Context Range**
Demonstrate adaptability across enterprise (Xerox, Apple, Verizon), startups (WAE), education (RIT, MCC), and community (Upstate UX, Crashtest). This reflects systems thinking refined through multiple lenses.

### 2. **Skill Range**
Your stack is multidimensional: UX, systems design, programming, automation, AI, workshops, leadership. Reinforce through outcomes, not task descriptions.

### 3. **Authority + Impact**
Establish trust by highlighting your decade-plus track record across brands and sectors, plus equity-driven community contributions and pro bono work.

## Page 1: **UX Portfolio + Consulting Gateway**

### Primary Use

- Used in UX job applications
- Showcases process, impact, and leadership
- Converts hiring managers, recruiters, or UX-adjacent founders into leads for:
  - Maturity audits
  - UX/Product leadership
  - Fractional or project-based roles

### Target Personas

- UX/Product hiring managers
- Recruiters (technical and non-technical)
- Team leads scaling design maturity
- Founders needing better team/process design

### Strategic Priorities

- Above-the-fold: credibility, clarity, quick wins
- Case studies show org type, before/after outcomes, system/process depth
- Services framed as **ops levers**, not design fluff
- Lead magnets include:
  - **UX Maturity Audit**
  - **Hiring Guide for Non-Technical Recruiters**
  - **UX Office Hours / Tear-Downs**
- CTAs guide different personas into either contact, free resource, or deeper page

### Navigation Behavior

- Internal link to “non-UX” landing page for SMB/founder ops work
- Content layered to speak to both personas (UX fluent and UX adjacent) without losing either

## Page 2: **Non-UX Operator / Founder Landing Page**

### Primary Use

- Converts small business operators and founders into clients for:
  - Systems/process automation
  - Quiet audits and workflow redesign
  - Fractional leadership and coaching

### Target Personas

- SMB owners/operators (overworked, under-digitized)
- Founders scaling quickly but breaking ops
- Non-technical decision-makers overwhelmed by tech stacks

### Strategic Priorities

- Empathetic, Voss-style copy: “You’re not broken, but your systems might be.”
- Avoid buzzwords; speak in outcomes: saved time, reduced errors, stress relief
- Lead magnet: **Quiet Fix Audit** (captures pain points via form/quiz)
- CTAs offer low-risk next steps:
  - Downloadable checklist
  - Free audit preview
  - Book a “quiet coaching” call

## Shared Tactics Across Pages

### High-Value Case Studies

Use WAE, Xerox, MCC, Upstate UX, and Crashtest to reflect:
- Range (startup to enterprise)
- System/process transformations
- Equity and leadership outcomes

### Visual Proof & Skimmable Trust

- Logos: Apple, Verizon, Xerox, PepsiCo, RIT
- Quote cards: Sean Boyle (RIT), Jenn Kotler (Space Telescope)
- Process visuals: before/after diagrams, UX maturity canvas, automation flow snapshots

### Lead Capture Assets

1. **UX Maturity Audit** – async, low-risk entry point for product/UX leaders
2. **Hiring Guide for Non-Tech Recruiters** – clarity tool for HR/staffing personas
3. **Quiet Fix Audit** – trust-building consult for overwhelmed SMB operators

## Writing & Tone

- Mix **Chris Voss** empathy hooks with **Carnegie-style warmth** and tactical specificity.
- Always lead with clarity and outcomes, then offer depth for those who want it.
- Use voice of an insider: "I’ve seen this pattern before—and fixed it.”

## Final Summary

- The **main UX page** acts as both portfolio and conversion gate.
- Visitors self-identify and are funneled to **persona-specific pages**.
- Everything builds compounding trust, authority, and revenue opportunity—without breaking momentum or requiring new builds per persona.


## Anatomy of a High-Converting Landing Page

- Clear headline and subheadline with benefit-focused messaging
- Immediate credibility (logos, client names, years of experience)
- Visual scannability (sections, icons, short blocks)
- Segmentation prompts if multiple personas apply
- Focused problem framing and audience targeting
- Simple CTA (email or lead magnet)
- Testimonials or social proof
- "Why me" with specific expertise claims
- Specific services with outcome framing
- Case studies or impact call-outs
- Optional lead magnet or content hook
- Reinforced final CTA
