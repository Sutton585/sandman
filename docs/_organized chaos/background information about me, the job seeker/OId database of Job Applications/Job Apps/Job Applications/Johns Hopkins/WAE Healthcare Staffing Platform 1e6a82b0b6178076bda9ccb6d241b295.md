## Project Context & Challenge

In a fast-paced startup environment, I led UX and product design for a healthcare staffing platform built to simplify job scheduling and order fulfillment. The original MVP (built by an external team) was expensive to develop, unattractive and difficult to use, and functionally inadequate. I was brought in to rebuild it from the ground up.

### **Our Goal**

Address post-COVID staffing instability and coordination inefficiencies in healthcare with an app to expedite staffing agency processes and facilitate direct connection between employers and medical professionals.

### **My Role**

Principal UX/Product hire. Responsible for design strategy, delivery, and foundational process-building while scaling up processes and design teams.

### The Challenge

- Launch two brands (Medical Staffing Org, General Staffing Org) and a functional MVP in under six months.
- No design systems, roadmaps, stakeholder alignment, workflows or delivery process.
- Collaboration across departments was informal and siloed. Engineering was scaling fast, but without any product leadership or shared vision; no roadmaps, requirements or timelines had been defined.

### **My Approach**

Build trust across teams and individual stakeholders, establish collaborative systems and processes while focusing our product strategy around user needs. My design team would set the bar appropriately by modeling the collaborative communication-driven operational standards we needed across departments.

# Strategic Process & Systems Design

### Tools & Documentation Systems

- Built centralized design system in **Figma**, applying atomic design principles. All design artifacts are transparent; individual modules, screens and interactive prototypes are all perfectly arranged by workflow.
- Created a **live documentation hub** that linked design assets, requirements, usage guidance, and test results. Everything is linked to related design system content, Jira tickets, and related material inside of documentation hub.
- Embedded usability testing and validation into sprint planning and product roadmapping.

### Framework & Methodology

I implemented a **dual-track agile model** to ensure continuous discovery and delivery while maintaining pace up with a rapidly scaling engineering team.

- **Discovery Track**: Workshops, interviews, rapid prototyping, competitive analysis.
- **Delivery Track**: Sprint-based iteration with continuous testing and feedback.

## Stakeholder Collaboration & Engagement

### Community & Client Involvement

For some of our most productive user-testing sessions, I involved real clients early, inviting them to test-drive prototypes for critical features and flows. 

This provided us with not only invaluable feedback from real users, but also strengthened relationships with our clients and advertised the innovations being developed to address their specific needs.

### Impact

Clients became strategic collaborators. Their input directly shaped user flows and prioritization.

- Built trust and excitement for the roadmap.
- Strengthened sales relationships and client retention.
- Received actionable, high-quality feedback grounded in real-world use.

## Reflection

This project demonstrates civic design in practice:

- Scaling systems in resource-constrained environments.
- Building transparent, participatory workflows.
- Centering both the user and the community in every decision.
    
    It's the kind of mission-driven, community-connected work I'm most proud of-and the type of public innovation I want to keep driving.
    

### Results

From the earliest stages of this project, I ensured a standard was maintained for soliciting feedback and collaboration from diverse stakeholders. This diversity of voices and perspectives allowed us a clearer view of our challenges and more informed strategy for our solutions. Simple initiatives like combining client outreach and early user testing yielded massive results. 

- Average user satisfaction scores quickly jumped from 52% to 96%.
- Users reported a 50% reduction in time to complete key tasks.
- Simplicity and efficiency of this design described as a “game changer” by users.
