# AI Adoption Manager
**Location:** Rochester, NY (Hybrid/Remote)
**Compensation:** $65,000 - $103,000 + ESOP & Bonus

## Summary
The AI Adoption Manager leads the full lifecycle of AI enablement across the agency—from initial rollout of enterprise AI tools to long-term optimization of advanced capabilities. This role bridges configuration, training, adoption, and governance to ensure Butler/Till extracts maximum value from its AI investments.

## Key Outcomes & Responsibilities
* **Enterprise Rollout:** Lead staged rollout of AI solutions (e.g., Microsoft Copilot); manage lifecycle from onboarding to optimization.
* **Functional Training:** Partner with Media Ops, Analytics, Creative, and Finance to configure role-specific AI bots, prompts, and workflows.
* **Performance Tracking:** Define adoption KPIs and quantify impact regarding productivity gains and error reduction.
* **Enablement:** Design role-based training programs; establish "always-on" resources (toolkits, office hours).
* **Governance:** Establish and lead an AI Council with executive sponsors; co-own data hygiene and ethics strategy.

## Qualifications
* **Required:** 3-5 years in software enablement, product ownership, or AI configuration.
* **Required:** Hands-on experience configuring/training AI tools, SaaS, or chatbots.
* **Preferred:** Background in marketing/advertising; expertise in Microsoft Copilot/Azure AI/OpenAI.

# AI Adoption Manager

## SUMMARY

The AI Adoption Manager leads the full lifecycle of AI enablement across the agency — from initial rollout of enterprise AI tools to long-term optimization of advanced capabilities. This role bridges configuration, training, adoption, and governance to ensure Butler/Till extracts maximum value from its AI investments.
In addition to driving training and adoption, this role plays a critical part in configuring AI bots and models to meet the unique needs of functional teams, balancing innovation with security, compliance, and risk management. Acting as both strategist and hands-on enabler, the AI Adoption Manager ensures that AI solutions evolve in step with business requirements and deliver measurable, ethical, and scalable impact.


## KEY OUTCOMES & RESPONSIBILITIES

## Key Outcome
Implement and manage the enterprise rollout and full lifecycle management of AI tools to ensure smooth deployment, adoption, and continuous optimization across the agency.


## Key Responsibilities:
- Lead the staged rollout of enterprise AI solutions (e.g., Microsoft Copilot, embedded AI in SaaS platforms).
- Manage the lifecycle from onboarding to adoption, feature expansion, and ongoing optimization.
- Partner with IT and Business Transformation to ensure seamless implementation and integration.
- Establish pilot groups across diverse functions to gather insights and refine rollout plans.
- Define, communicate, and manage the AI rollout timeline with clear milestones and deliverables.
- Track and report on pilot outcomes, using data and feedback to inform future rollouts and ongoing optimization.
- Strategically assign AI licenses to high-impact teams and priority use cases during initial rollout.
- Monitor license utilization and continuously optimize allocation to ensure maximize business value and ROI.
- Define adoption and utilization KPIs, tracking across functions.
- Quantify impact in terms of productivity gains, error reduction, and quality improvements.
- Align metrics with business outcomes, focusing on productivity, efficiency, quality, and employee experience.
- Continuously evolve AI usage to align with business and client needs.

## Key Outcome:
- Configure and train AI bots and models to deliver accurate, role-specific, and business-aligned outputs across departments.


## Key Responsibilities:

- Serve as the functional “trainer” of AI bots and models to meet specific departmental needs (e.g., Media Ops, Analytics, Creative, Finance).
- Configure AI settings, prompts, workflows, and integrations to ensure relevant, high-quality outputs.
- Maintain AI system accuracy by curating datasets, refining configurations, and managing contextual understanding.
- Partner with IT and data governance to ensure configurations meet security and compliance standards.

## Key Outcome
- Drive agency-wide AI Adoption through training, enablement, and continuous learning

## Key Responsibilities:
- Design and deliver role-based training programs, ensuring confidence and compliance in AI use.
- Establish “always-on” enablement resources including toolkits, office hours, and user communities.
- Build and sustain a Copilot user community to encourage peer learning and best practices.
- Identify and empower AI champions and early adopters to promote engagement and support.
- Facilitate feedback loops and document use cases to showcase success and innovation across functions.
- Implement structured feedback mechanisms (surveys, focus groups, direct channels) to inform ongoing improvements.

## Key Outcome
- Drive organizational readiness and engagement through effective change management and transparent communicatio
## Key Responsibilities:
- Establish and lead an AI Council with executive sponsors and cross-functional stakeholders, to guide strategy, adoption, and governance.
- Develop and execute a comprehensive change management plan that includes proactive communication, role-based training, and clear expectation setting.
- Foster a culture of trust, transparency, and continuous learning around AI adoption.
- Build trust and transparency by addressing resistance, clarifying AI’s role, and fostering a culture of continuous learning.

## Key Outcome
-  Strengthen data privacy, governance and hygiene to ensure secure, compliant, and ethical AI usage

## Key Responsibilities:
- Co-own the enterprise data hygiene strategy, collaborating with IT and data teams to ensure quality, accessibility, and compliance.
- Review, enforce, and continuously improve data governance policies, focusing on security, privacy, regulatory compliance, and ethical use.
- Conduct regular audits of data sources, permissions, and AI outputs for accuracy and mitigate risk.
- Balance configuration flexibility with robust data security, and brand safety requirements.
- Monitor user behavior, escalate potential risks, and enforce enterprise AI usage policies.
