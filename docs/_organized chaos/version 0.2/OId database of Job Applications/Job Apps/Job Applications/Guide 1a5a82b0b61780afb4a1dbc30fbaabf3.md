**WHO ARE WE?**

We are Atmosfy - think TikTok for your city, where 10,000 cities have their own dedicated video feeds and local communities. We're powering discovery of over 150 million experiences and the fastest-growing platform for discovery of dining, nightlife, & travel.

And simply put, our community loves Atmosfy! We're top #100 on the App Store, have 3M+ businesses on the platform, and are scaling in 10,000 cities.

**ABOUT THE ROLE:**

As a **UX/UI Designer** at **Atmosfy**, you will play a pivotal role in crafting engaging and intuitive mobile experiences that delight our users. Your expertise will guide the design process from initial concept through final implementation, ensuring our app is both visually appealing and user-friendly.

**WHAT YOU'LL BE DOING?**

**User Research & Analysis:**

- Conduct user research to understand target audiences, their behaviors, and needs.
- Develop user personas and journey maps to inform design decisions.

**UX Design:**

- Create wireframes, prototypes, and user flows to define app structure and navigation.
- Ensure designs are optimized for usability, accessibility, and responsiveness.

**UI Design:**

- Design visually compelling interfaces aligned with Atmosfy's brand identity.
- Develop and maintain a cohesive style guide and design system.

**Collaboration:**

- Work closely with product managers, developers, and other stakeholders to ensure seamless design implementation.
- Participate in brainstorming sessions and provide creative solutions to design challenges.

**Usability Testing & Iteration:**

- Conduct usability tests to gather feedback and iterate on designs.
- Analyze user feedback and data to inform design improvements.

**WHAT WER'E LOOKING FOR:**

- 2+ years of experience as UX/UI Designer
- Bachelor’s degree in Design, Human-Computer Interaction, or a related field.
- Proven experience as a UX/UI Designer with a strong portfolio showcasing mobile app design projects.
- Proficiency in design tools such as Sketch, Figma, or Adobe XD.
- Strong understanding of user-centered design principles and best practices.
- Excellent communication and collaboration skills.
- Ability to manage multiple projects and meet deadlines in a fast-paced environment.

At Atmosfy, we value creativity, innovation, and a passion for delivering exceptional user experiences. If you're excited about shaping the future of our mobile app and making a tangible impact, we'd love to hear from you.

### Resume Creation

### Title

What is the target job title? What are one or two related titles that show my additional value to this role?

In this role, it seems to be looking for UX Designer with around two years experience. The job description has a few key areas: 

- **User Research & Analysis**
- **UX Design**
- **UI Design**
- **Collaboration**
- **Usability Testing & Iteration**

We always use the target job title first, so that takes care of UX Design. It’s the target job title, it’s a focus of the job description, and the first requirement says “2+ years of experience as UX/UI Designer”, so let’s include UI in there as well. How can we combine the others, or incorporate other important ATS keywords for this role?

We can do these three:

UI/UX Design, User Research and Testing, Collaborative Development

### Professional Summary

Professional summary is our primary sales-pitch. The first place on the resume that the reader reads.

Often theres some guesswork, but in this example, we are lucky to receive clear hints about what they’re looking for in this section, we can use it for how to portray their ideal match:

> **WHO ARE YOU?**
> 
> 
> Motivated, self-managing, and think outside the box. You love a new challenge and are excited to help us redefine how people discover experiences all over the world while supporting local businesses. You fancy yourself something of a dining, nightlife, and travel aficionado. 
> 

Some keywords there: Motivated, self-managing, “outside the box”, excited, “love a challenge”, “support local businesses”

Here’s another section right after:

> **ABOUT THE ROLE:**
> 
> 
> As a **UX/UI Designer** at **Atmosfy**, you will play a pivotal role in crafting engaging and intuitive mobile experiences that delight our users. Your expertise will guide the design process from initial concept through final implementation, ensuring our app is both visually appealing and user-friendly.
> 

Key ideas and phrases: “crafting engaging and intuitive mobile experiences”, “user delight”, “guide the design process”, “visually appealing”, “user friendly”.

### Creating the Summary

1. First sentence starts with target job title, then two alternate job titles to show value and hit ATS keywords, show desired motivation for the role. Other than the first one, these do not need to be the same as the three listed before, we are trying to hit all relevant ATS keywords we’ve seen. 
    1. In this example, instead of UI/UX Designer, I altered the naming slightly to ensure I am ranking for the job title and likely alternate keywords for it that might be measured by ATS, and other important keywords from job description.
    
    ---
    
    > Outside-the-box User Experience Designer specializing in mobile-first UX/UI, usability testing, and design systems.
    > 
2. Second sentence establishes experience and credibility, shows the appropriate years of experience, and mentions recognizable brand names I’ve worked with (Apple, Xerox, Verizon)
    1. Normally, I’d say “over ten years experience” in this sentence, but in this example, they’re looking for someone with two years experience, so I will change the phrasing to avoid looking too over-qualified.
    
    > Years of experience crafting engaging and intuitive and intuitive experiences, user research, and interaction design for companies including Apple, Xerox, Verizon, and PepsiCo.
    > 
3. Then the rest of the summary hits the top priority skills and experience keywords from the target job description, almost as a summary of the job description in a few sentences
    
    in this example, we know there’s these key areas they are looking for:
    
    - **User Research & Analysis**
    - **UX Design**
    - **UI Design**
    - **Collaboration**
    - **Usability Testing & Iteration**
    
    We’ve already talked about UI and UX Design, so we should end the summary with the other priorities: User Research & Analysis, Collaboration, Usability Testing & Iteration while hitting relevant keywords.

### Specific Section

This is a more junior role, so we might not need the additional section, but for jobs looking for someone with more experience, we can include another section to show our specific area of focus that might not have been included very well in the original focus of the resume.

For example, for a design leadership role with a focus on training and developing the team and involving stakeholders in brainstorming and collaboration, we might want to include a section like this:

## Community Leadership & Facilitation

- **Founder, Upstate UX** – Established a UX training and professional networking group hosting workshops, panel discussions, and community design discussion.
- **Founder, Crashtest (RIT)** – Developed an interactive user-testing and research initiative that became a significant part of the RIT curriculum.
- **Teaching & Instruction** – Designed and led UX, design thinking, and innovation workshops at both college and high school levels.
- **Certified Facilitator** – Earned IDEO & LUMA certifications in **collaborative design, rapid iteration, and user research methods**.
- **Public Speaking & Mentorship** – Frequent speaker at UX and community design events, mentoring emerging professionals and students.

### Body

This is our actual sales pitch to get invited to an interview. We don’t have a lot of time or space to make a long arguement, we need to essentially introduce ourselves, demonstrate authority and credibility in the releveant areas of expertise while also displaying the right attitude. This is going to be quickly skimmed, so it’s essentially like we get to do a laser-targetted professional summary that ends in a call to action to get them to reach out to schedule an interview.

Just like the professional summary, we have to quickly show interest in the role and show our own authority, expertise and credibility.

---

Mr. Ebel (or Hiring Manager), 

I am excited to apply for the UX/UI Designer position at Atmosfy. With a strong background in user research, wireframing, and interactive prototyping, I have spent over a decade crafting seamless digital experiences for companies like Apple, Xerox, Verizon, and PepsiCo. My expertise in mobile-first UX, usability testing, and design systems aligns well with Atmosfy’s mission to create engaging and intuitive customer experiences that redefine how people discover experiences all over the world. 

In my previous roles, I have:

- Led UX research and usability testing to gather actionable insights, informing design decisions that enhanced user engagement and satisfaction.
- Designed and iterated mobile-first experiences, ensuring accessibility, responsiveness, and a frictionless user journey.
- Developed scalable design systems and UI components, fostering consistency across products and accelerating development workflows.
- Collaborated cross-functionally with product managers, developers, and stakeholders to align design strategies with business goals and user needs.

Atmosfy’s vision of redefining how users discover and engage with local experiences deeply resonates with me. My ability to balance user-centered design with business objectives will enable me to contribute meaningfully to your growing platform.

I welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience can support Atmosfy’s UX evolution. Please find my resume attached. I look forward to hearing from you.
