#### Dear Hiring Manager,

## About Me

I’ve spent my career helping organizations build creative systems that scale—whether that’s design standards, onboarding kits, or tools that reduce the back-and-forth. I care most about removing friction from the design process so teams can stay focused on what matters. My background spans UX/CX and creative strategy, visual systems, campaign tooling, and creative ops for healthcare, biotech, and public-facing orgs. I like figuring out how things work, spotting inefficiencies, and designing the infrastructure to support better work with less chaos.

## Why USP?

My connection to USP goes back a long time. My father, Scott Sutton, was a contributing expert to several USP projects. My family has always had respect for the kind of rigor and mission-first clarity USP stands for. That combination of scientific integrity and design consistency is rare, and it's something I value. I’d be excited to support a team where clarity, accessibility, and trust in communication are treated as core to the work.

## Why Me?

I’m not the kind of design manager that chases awards. I’m more interested in making sure the right assets are in the right place, used by the right people, and easy to update when things change. I’ve built templates, DAMs, and motion graphics systems to help teams deliver high-quality work under pressure. I’ve worked in regulated environments and know how to balance creativity with governance. If you need someone who can make design easier for everyone around them, that’s where I fit best.
